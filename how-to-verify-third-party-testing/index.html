How to verify third-party testing

Verifying third-party testing starts with document access. If the public page does not lead to a readable report or COA, the claim stays incomplete.

After that, the key questions are who performed the testing, which batch the file belongs to, and whether the dates look current.

Step 1

Open the supporting file

Access

A verification process cannot begin if the page never links to the document it references.

Step 2

Check the laboratory context

Source

Look for a lab header, report identity, or another sign that the document has a clear origin.

Step 3

Match the batch

Fit

The document should feel tied to the specific listing instead of functioning as a floating generic file.

Step 4

Review the date and method

Support

The stronger claim is the one that is both dated and explained clearly enough to read.

Verification habits

A testing claim is only as useful as the public support behind it

The phrase third-party tested can sound decisive, but a visitor still has to look at the file, the dates, and the labeling around it.

A site that makes those steps easy to complete is usually easier to evaluate overall.
