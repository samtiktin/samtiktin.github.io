How to read a peptide COA

A readable COA gives more than a purity headline. It should connect the batch, test date, and lab information in a way that makes the document easier to verify against the product page.

What is a COA? Browse suppliers
Field 1

Batch or lot reference

Traceability

The document should show a batch or lot reference that feels tied to the product page rather than a floating generic file.

Field 2

Test date

Timing

Dates help show whether the COA looks current, archived, or disconnected from the listing being presented.

Field 3

Method and result

Clarity

A stronger COA explains what was measured and how the result was recorded, instead of dropping a number without context.

Field 4

Lab identity

Context

Visitors usually want to know whether the document includes a named lab, report header, or other identifiable context.

Quick checklist

What to compare first

  • Whether the batch ID matches the listing.
  • Whether the test date is visible and readable.
  • Whether the purity result is shown with method context.
  • Whether the file is easy to open without hunting through the site.
Common weak spots

What makes a COA less useful

  • Files that look reused across many products.
  • Purity claims without visible test timing.
  • Hard-to-read scans with missing headers.
  • Product pages that mention testing but never link the document clearly.
Keep reading

Use the document together with the page.

A COA is easiest to judge when the product page, batch details, and surrounding site language all stay consistent. That is why it helps to compare the document and the listing side by side.
