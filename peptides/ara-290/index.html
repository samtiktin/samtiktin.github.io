Overview

Overview

ARA-290, also known as cibinetide, is an 11-amino-acid peptide derived from erythropoietin research. It was engineered to activate tissue-protective pathways without stimulating red blood cell production in the same way as erythropoietin. This distinction is central to understanding the compound.

ARA-290 is most commonly discussed in small fiber neuropathy, tissue-protection, inflammation, and innate repair receptor research. Human studies have evaluated it in conditions such as sarcoidosis-associated small fiber neuropathy and diabetic small fiber neuropathy.

For PeptideSuppliers.org, ARA-290 should be positioned as a tissue-protective and neuropathy research peptide, not as a general recovery peptide. It has a more specific scientific identity than many thin directory pages suggest. The best page should explain its erythropoietin-derived background, innate repair receptor mechanism, and human neuropathy research.

Research snapshot

Quick research reference

Research Category: Tissue-protective, neuropathy, and innate repair receptor research.

Research Status: Human clinical research exists, but the compound remains investigational in many contexts.

Common Areas Studied: Sarcoidosis-associated small fiber neuropathy, diabetic small fiber neuropathy, neuropathic pain, tissue repair, and inflammatory injury models.

Related Compounds: BPC-157, TB-500, KPV, GHK-Cu, thymosin beta-4, and other tissue-repair research peptides.
