Research Areas

The main research domains where this compound appears most often.

The main research area for CJC-1295 is GH/IGF-1 axis stimulation. Studies have examined GH release, IGF-1 increases, half-life, and duration of endocrine response.

A second research area is pharmacokinetics. CJC-1295 is valuable because it illustrates how peptide engineering can extend duration through albumin binding. This makes it relevant to broader peptide-drug design discussions.

CJC-1295 is also a comparison compound. It is commonly compared with sermorelin, tesamorelin, and ipamorelin. A high-quality page should explain the receptor and duration differences between these compounds.

Animal studies

What preclinical work has emphasized

Animal studies have evaluated CJC-1295 in models involving GHRH deficiency and growth. These models help illustrate the ability of long-acting GHRH analogs to stimulate GH and IGF-1 signaling over an extended period.

The limitation is that animal growth models do not translate directly into adult human body-composition outcomes. They are best used to explain mechanism and endocrine duration rather than broad claims.

Human studies

How much human evidence exists

Human studies of CJC-1295 have evaluated pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, and endocrine effects. Research reported prolonged increases in growth hormone and IGF-1, helping define CJC-1295 as a long-acting GHRH analog.

The human evidence is important but not broad. It should be summarized as endocrine and pharmacology research rather than proof of wide-ranging benefits. Dose, population, monitoring, and study design all affect interpretation.

Current research status

Where the research stands now

CJC-1295 remains important in peptide education as a long-acting GHRH analog. Its research value is strongest for understanding peptide half-life extension, albumin binding, and GH/IGF-1 axis stimulation.

Current interest is mostly comparative and mechanistic. Readers often need help understanding how CJC-1295 differs from sermorelin, tesamorelin, ipamorelin, and growth hormone.

Supplier considerations

How to read supplier pages more carefully

CJC-1295 supplier listings should clearly state whether the compound is CJC-1295 with DAC or a non-DAC GHRH analog. Researchers should look for COAs, purity data, sequence identity, lot numbers, storage instructions, and research-use labeling. Naming clarity is especially important for this compound.

  • Look for batch-specific COAs instead of generic laboratory files reused across many listings.
  • Check whether the product name, concentration language, and batch references stay consistent from page to page.
  • Read storage and handling notes alongside the document links rather than treating the headline purity claim as enough.
  • Prefer supplier pages that keep research-use labeling, contact details, and policy pages easy to verify.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions drawn from the published literature and documentation context.

What is CJC-1295?

CJC-1295 is a synthetic long-acting GHRH analog studied for stimulating GH and IGF-1.

What does DAC mean?

DAC refers to the drug-affinity complex design associated with albumin binding and extended duration.

Is CJC-1295 the same as sermorelin?

No. Both are GHRH-related, but CJC-1295 was designed for longer duration.

How is CJC-1295 different from ipamorelin?

CJC-1295 activates GHRH receptors, while ipamorelin activates ghrelin receptors.

Does CJC-1295 have human research?

Yes. Human studies have evaluated pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, and GH/IGF-1 response.

What is CJC-1295 studied for?

It is studied for GH release, IGF-1 elevation, half-life extension, and endocrine signaling.

Why is naming important with CJC-1295?

Some suppliers use CJC-1295 to refer to different GHRH analog versions, so documentation matters.

What should researchers check when comparing suppliers?

COAs, sequence identity, DAC/no-DAC labeling, purity data, and storage guidance.
