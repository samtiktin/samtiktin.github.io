Good signs

What strong supplier pages tend to do

  • Put documentation links where readers expect them.
  • Keep labels and product names consistent.
  • Use shipping and policy pages that match the listings.
  • Make the catalog easy to browse without hiding the basics.
Useful next step

Use the checklist beside a live page

The checklist works best when used on a real supplier page. Open a listing, compare the details one by one, and see whether the page earns trust through clarity rather than just presentation.
